BQSKit at ASPLOS 2026: AlphaSyndrome and QTurbo

April 1, 2026 · 3 min read

We're excited to announce that two BQSKit papers were accepted to ASPLOS 2026, the International Conference on Architectural Support for Programming Languages and Operating Systems — one of the top venues in computer architecture and systems.

AlphaSyndrome

AlphaSyndrome addresses syndrome measurement circuit scheduling for fault-tolerant quantum computing. In surface codes and other quantum error correcting codes, syndrome measurements must be performed repeatedly to detect errors without measuring the logical state. Scheduling these measurements efficiently is critical for minimizing the overhead introduced by error correction.

AlphaSyndrome formulates this as an optimization problem and applies BQSKit's compilation infrastructure to produce optimized syndrome measurement schedules, reducing the circuit depth required for fault-tolerant operation.

QTurbo

QTurbo tackles scalable circuit optimization for wide circuits — those spanning 60 or more qubits. BQSKit's existing QGO pass already uses partitioning + synthesis for global optimization, but QTurbo pushes this further with a new partitioning strategy that significantly improves throughput on large circuits.

The key insight in QTurbo is that the partitioning and synthesis schedule can be dynamically adapted based on circuit structure, enabling better gate cancellation across partition boundaries.
