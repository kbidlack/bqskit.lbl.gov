Research & Publications

Six research programs.
Spanning the full compilation stack.

BQSKit is a research compiler. These are the areas we publish in, with links to the underlying papers.

Research Areas

Active programs.

Each area has associated publications in the full list below.

01
Domain-Level Optimization
Hamiltonian-level compilation (HATT), constant-depth TFIM circuits, and application-specific simulation circuit generation.
HATT: Fermion-to-Qubit Mapping · HPCA 2025 Constant-Depth TFIM Circuits · Materials Theory 2022 Ground State Energy (Szasz et al.) · Quantum 2025
02
Gate-Set Independent Optimization
Hardware-agnostic transpilation, scalable synthesis (QSearch ≤4q, LEAP ≤6q, QFAST ≤8q, QGO to 60+ qubits), routing & mapping, classical optimizers for NISQ/VQE.
★ QFAST · QCE 2021 Best Paper ★ Topology-Aware Synthesis · QCE 2020 Best Paper LEAP · ACM TQC 2022 Permutation-Aware Mapping · QCE 2023
03
AI-Driven Scalability
Machine learning approaches to synthesis, plus QFactor — a tensor-network-based instantiation optimizer that processes 12+ qubit circuits directly and enables 100+ qubit optimization pipelines in BQSKit.
Improving Synthesis with ML · QCE 2023 QFactor Domain-Specific Optimizer · QCE 2023
04
Fault-Tolerant Synthesis
High-precision Clifford+T synthesis via unitary diagonalization, and application-scale compilation with controlled error budgets.
High-Precision Clifford+T Synthesis · QPL 2025 Application-Scale FT Compilation · arXiv 2025
05
Controlling Error & Approximation
Empirical evaluation of approximations on real noisy hardware (SC 2021), and QUEST — robust resource-efficient circuit approximation (ASPLOS 2022).
Empirical Circuit Approximations · SC 2021 QUEST · ASPLOS 2022
06
QEC Implementation
Lattice surgery compilation (TopoLS), magic state scheduling, and syndrome measurement scheduling (AlphaSyndrome, ASPLOS 2026).
TopoLS: Lattice Surgery Compilation · arXiv 2026 AlphaSyndrome · ASPLOS 2026
Open Problems

Active research challenges.

Hard problems we're working on — useful for collaborators, students, and potential contributors.

P1
Multi-Device / Distributed Compilation
Communication-minimizing partitioning and scheduling for circuits that span multiple QPUs.
P2
Learning-Based Layout for Dynamic Architectures
Layout and routing that adapts to changing hardware topologies between calibration cycles without full recompilation.
P3
Scalable QEC Compilation Beyond Surface Codes
Efficient compilation for color codes, bivariate bicycle codes, and other QEC families beyond the well-studied surface code lattice surgery methods.
P4
Real-Time Compilation for Hybrid Loops
Low-latency adaptive compilation for variational algorithms, compatible with quantum coherence timescales. BQSKit is already used for real-time QITE optimization at AQT.
P5
Full-Stack Fault-Tolerant Compilation
End-to-end compilation from logical circuit to physical lattice surgery schedule — incorporating magic state management and syndrome scheduling — in one coherent pipeline.
